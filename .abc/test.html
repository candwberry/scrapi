Your Progression Status

When you view your marks, underneath it will state what your progression status is, which will inform you of what outcome the exam board determined for you based on your mark profile:

  • Activate – progress to next level or Progress Manually – Historical Record Incomplete: The examination board confirmed that you can progress to the next level of your degree programme, with no further action required.
  • Activate pending resit results: The examination board determined that in order to progress in to the next level of your programme, you will first be required to complete at least one resit (more information below), and your progression cannot be confirmed until early September pending the outcome of the resit(s).
  • Review: At present, the examination board was unable to confirm your progression. If you are unable to progress or have further action to take before doing so, you will be sent a separate email informing you of this.

Understanding your results

If you are required to take any resits or have had any compensation applied to your marks you will be able to see this when you view your marks. The below table explains what the marks and resit notes mean:

Explanation of Resit Notes

Resit Note Explanation
C - Compensated course unit For this course unit, you have received a compensated pass having achieved a mark of 30% - 39%. Compensation is indicated by the suffix "C" after the mark (e.g. "32C") and indicates you have passed the course without the need to re-sit - no further action is required.
R1 - Resit exam as a first attempt Due to accepted mitigating circumstances for this course unit, you are required to take a deferral exam in the August exam period. Your mark for this resit will not be capped and will be inclusive of any coursework that has already been submitted.
R2 - Resit exam as a second attempt You have failed this course unit; you are required to take a referral re-sit exam in the August exam period. Your mark for this resit will be capped at a mark of 30 (or the original mark you achieved if this is above 30), and the mark will be based on the resit assessment only.
A1 - Resubmit coursework as a first attempt Due to accepted mitigating circumstances for this course unit, you are required to complete a deferral coursework assignment. Your mark for this resit will not be capped and will be inclusive of all component marks for the course unit.
A2 - Resubmit coursework as a second attempt You have failed this course unit; you are required to complete a referral coursework assignment. Your mark for this resit will be capped at a mark of 30 (or the original mark you achieved if this is above 30), and the mark will be based on the resit assignment only.
30R Recorded as unit mark For this course unit, you have failed due to a late submission penalty being applied to your mark. Your mark for the unit has automatically been capped at 30R and you will not be required to do the re-sit.

Right to Appeal

If you think there are adequate grounds for an appeal against a decision of the examination board, in the first instance, please refer to the University's Appeals Procedure (Academic Appeals – Regulation XIX). Please read the documentation carefully.

If you wish to pursue an appeal, your first step would be to submit a Stage 1 appeal, which will be sent directly to the school for consideration. You will then receive an outcome from the school, and can then proceed to Stage 2 if your Stage 1 appeal is unsuccessful. A Stage 2 appeal would instead be reviewed by the Faculty of Biology, Medicine and Health Appeals, Complaints and Discipline team.

Please submit your Stage 1 appeal through this online form. The form will tell you what information you need to provide. You cannot submit a Stage 2 appeal until the school has already considered a Stage 1 appeal.

Please Note:

  • Your Stage 1 appeal must be submitted within 20 working days of the date you were informed of the decision you wish to appeal against, e.g. when your results are released. Any appeal received outside of this timeframe will be automatically rejected.
  • Evidence (e.g. medical evidence) must be provided to support the appeal. Any appeal that does not provide evidence will not be considered.
  • We will not consider any appeals which challenge academic judgment or are in relation to generally being unhappy with a mark that you have been awarded.
  • If you appealing on the grounds of mitigating circumstances, your appeal must evidence why you did not submit an application for mitigating circumstances to the school prior to the examination board.

If you have any generic appeal-related queries, please direct these to sbs.appealsandconduct@manchester.ac.uk. Please note we will not accept appeals submitted to this address, they must be submitted through the form linked above.

The Students' Union Advice Service also provides useful information on the appeals process and can provide advice and guidance.
